1. The Architecture of the Unseen: The Folk Devil as Infrastructure
In forensic social analysis, the "Folk Devil" is not an accidental victim of circumstance but a  load-bearing component of social infrastructure . The Folk Devil does not merely receive the community’s fear; they produce the community’s unity.This mechanism is adaptive for low-trust communities for three primary reasons:
* Solidarity at Zero Transaction Cost:  Rival factions who cannot agree on territory or resources find "free consensus" by jointly orienting against an outsider, requiring no concessions or internal trust-building.
* Negative Solidarity:  By identifying what the community is  not , members reinforce shared identity through the collective act of condemnation—a "negative solidarity" that is the only available cohesion resource.
* Load-Bearing Social Cohesion:  The target absorbs the community's internal frictions and "cohesion deficits," allowing the group to function as a unified body without addressing its own underlying instability.The "So What?":  The target’s actual behavior is largely irrelevant. They are selected based on "availability" and "other-coding"—markers like housing status or unfamiliarity—that allow the group to use them as a tool for stabilization. The goal is not justice; it is the production of a common enemy to buy social oxygen.Once the need for a "villain" is established, the system requires raw material to process into a weapon.
